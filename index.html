    Machine Learning Image Recognition


    For this project, I experimented with the TensorFlow and ml5 libraries to make a couple image recognition programs. To learn the basics, I sourced some simple models and programs and created a simple program that reads in an image file and, using a pre-trained convolutional neural network, classifies that image and prints the certainty values returned by the neural net. Then I integrated the webcam into the program to create two other programs: another image recognition program and a position tracker.

    The goal of this project was to familiarize myself with basic machine learning libraries and get a baseline understanding on convolutional neural networks. Rather than simpy experimenting on convolutional neural networks in a simple program, I chose to integrate them into a web application that could be used more easily. I wanted to look more into the applications of machine learning rather than just trying to learn about the theory behind them.

    Ludum Dare 47: LoopGame


    For this project, I worked in a team of five to develop a game for Ludum Dare 47. Ludum Dare is a game design hackathon where participants are challenged to develop a video game within a couple days around a theme. The theme for this challenge was “Endless Loop”. We ended up making a roguelike game where the player travels around in a looped dungeon. The player decides the enemies they want to face in each room but, on each loop, the enemies you previously selected remain. The player fights to increase their score and gain upgrades to add to their monster slaying arsenal. But, as the player grows stronger, so do the enemies.

    In my team I acted as lead designer and scripter. I focused on creating the game environment and implementing the necessary systems to maximize player experience. I developed AI scripts for enemies with A* pathfinder, worked on the central game logic, and built the world from scratch. I also developed most of the sprites and animations for enemies and the world. The game ended up received strong positive feedback for art, innovative design, and complexity.

    Research: Using Wireless Signals for Mapping Indoor Environments


    Over the Summer of 2018, I participated in a high school research internship at the Rutgers Wireless Information Network Laboratory. I did research under Prof. Yingying Chen on using wireless signals for mapping indoor locations. I worked directly under graduate student Jian Liu with my main job being working on data management and analysis.     Huaxia South Chinese School

    Teacher Assistant Program Organizer/Founder


      While volunteering at my local Chinese School, I noticed that a lot of the teachers at the school were volunteers and had minimal experience teaching younger children. Many of them required assistance managing their students while also focusing on teaching and getting the appropriate material. So, me and a couple friends at the school decided to start a TA organization at the school where high school students could get volunteer hours assisting the teachers. Students could help out with classes that they were experienced with, whether it were sports, academics, art, or any other subject taught at the school, and teachers could lessen their work load and better focus on teaching.

       My main job in the organization was organizing the TAs and making sure all of them were assigned in a way that best fit their schedules and interests. I would log assistant's hours and communicate with teachers to make sure TAs were doing their job.
