Our Mission

Stop dehydration

Our goal with this product is to help people keep tabs on their hydration and make sure that they're water intake is within recommended guidelines. Nowadays, people are putting a lot more emphasis on physical fitness and exercise. However, some studies show that nearly 50% of Americans don't consume the recommended amount of water. In order to prevent the health risks of dehydration, we have developed our product as a way to help people track their water intake, maximize their performance capabilities, and improve their well-being.

Room for Improvement

What can we look to make better?

Our final product is still has some problems and lacks some features we would want to fix moving forward with this project. First of all, the cost of one transmitting and one receiving arduino could be cut in half by just implementing a bluetooth module to facilitate data collection directly to the server. Secondly, we would try to make the battery a rechargable one rather than a 9V. And finally, at the very late stages of development, we could try to implement AI to adjust to drinking patterns and tell you the most optimal time to drink.

Assembly/Planning

Here are the sketches that demonstrate the pieces that make up the our project.

For our bottle, we settled on using an ultrasonic sensor connected to an Arduino UNO to calculate the distance to the surface of the water, which could then be used to calculate the volume of water. All the circuitry is stored in a small box underneath the bottle and is powered by a 9V battery. The data would be sent to a server database that would provide to information to our web app.

Project Layout


Bottle Layout


Calculations

The sensor sends us the time for the sound waves to hit the surface of the water and bounce back. The rest of the calculations have to be done by the program.

Distance=(speed of sound) * (time) / 2

Height of water = (Height of bottle) - (distance)

Volume of water = pi * (radius of bottle)^2 * (Height of water)


Circuitry

Here is how the hardware was set up

In order to wireless send data from the bottle to our application, we decided to use 2 Arduino UNOs that would communicated via wireless transmitters and receivers. One Arduino would take the sensor data, perform the necessary calculations to find the volume of water, then wireless transmit the data. The other Arduino would then receive that data and send it to the computer to upload to our online database.

PROGRAMMING

Here is all of our code

Our code is separated into Arduino code using Java and web app code using HTML/CSS/Javascript

Arduino Program

We have two separate arduino programs for the transmitter and receiver. The transmitter performs the calculations to convert sensor data to distance then sends the data to the receiver.

Web App

The web app pulls user data from back end servers and displays personal weekly data. It keeps track of personal goals and reminds the user when they haven't drank.
